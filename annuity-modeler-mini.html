How to think about the diff

Forget the magic "number" the financial industry obsesses over. You don't retire on a balance — you retire on a cash flow. The question is whether your monthly spending will be reliably covered, or whether your portfolio has to scramble for it every month.

The diff is what's left after Social Security and any pension. Today, that gap usually lives in your portfolio's hands — and the market's mood. It doesn't have to.

The PILL framework

Four guarantees. In writing.

An annuity isn't a forecast. It's a contract — backed by an insurance company's reserves and your state's guaranty association.

P
Principal protection
Cannot decrease due to market losses. Not in the worst quarter. Not in the worst decade.
I
Income for life
A specific dollar amount, every month, for as long as you're alive. Promised.
L
Legacy
What's left goes to the people you choose. Named beneficiaries, no probate.
L
Long-term care
Monthly payment doubles if you need care. Built into the contract.
The actual contract clauses
Principal protection
"Account value will not decrease due to negative index performance."
Income for life
"Lifetime income payments will continue for the life of the annuitant."
Legacy
"Death benefit paid directly to designated beneficiaries."
Long-term care
"Income payments increase 2× upon qualifying care event (2 of 6 ADLs or cognitive impairment)."

No Monte Carlo. No "84% chance." Just four guarantees, in writing, signed.
