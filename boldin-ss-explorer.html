How to read chance of success and estate value

Chance of success is the probability that your plan fully covers your spending across your planning horizon. It is modeled year-by-year using your plan's assumptions: SS income reduces required withdrawals from your other assets, and a higher monthly floor means a more durable plan. Delaying improves this metric by reducing drawdown pressure during the critical early-retirement sequence-of-returns window.

Estate value is the projected net worth remaining at your planning age — what might remain for heirs. It is endpoint-sensitive: it can favor early claiming in short-horizon projections because SS income flows in sooner. It is included for context, not as a primary decision driver. The right question is not "which strategy leaves more at age 88?" — it is "which strategy keeps guaranteed income high enough if either of us lives to 95?"

For educational and illustrative purposes only. Chance of success and estate value are derived from your plan's assumptions and use simplified deterministic projections that do not account for inflation, taxes, or sequence-of-returns risk.
